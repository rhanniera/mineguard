Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about hazard reporting

How do I report a hazard?

Reporting a hazard is simple! Log into your account, navigate to the "Report Hazard" section, and fill out the form with details about the hazard you've observed. Include the title, description, location, severity level, date, and time. You can also attach supporting files. Once submitted, your report will be reviewed by our safety team.

Is my report confidential?

Yes! All hazard reports are treated with strict confidentiality. Your personal information is encrypted and protected. If you prefer, you can also choose to report anonymously, which will not display your name in the system. All reports are handled according to industry safety standards and privacy regulations.

How are hazards handled after reporting?

Once you submit a hazard report, it will be reviewed by our safety team. The report status will progress through "Pending" → "In Review" → "Resolved". You can track the status on your dashboard. Critical hazards are prioritized and addressed immediately. All hazards are assessed according to severity and appropriate corrective actions are taken.

Can I edit my report after submission?

Yes! You can edit your report as long as it hasn't been resolved. Simply go to your dashboard, find the report you want to edit, and click the edit button. You can update any information, add additional details, or attach more files. Once resolved, reports cannot be edited but you can add comments.

What if I need to report an emergency situation?

If you encounter a life-threatening or critical emergency situation, immediately:

  • 1. Evacuate the area and notify on-site supervisors
  • 2. Contact emergency services (911 or local equivalent)
  • 3. Report as "Critical" in MineGuard for immediate escalation

Your safety comes first - don't wait for system processing in emergencies.

Do I need a company account to report hazards?

Yes, you need to create a personal account with MineGuard to submit hazard reports. This ensures accountability, allows us to track hazard trends, and enables us to provide you with status updates. You'll need to provide your full name, company name, job role, and email address. The signup process takes less than 2 minutes.

What information should I include in my hazard report?

A comprehensive hazard report should include:

  • • Clear hazard title
  • • Detailed description of what you observed
  • • Specific location with coordinates if possible
  • • Accurate severity assessment
  • • Date and time observed
  • • Any supporting photos or documents

Hazard Reporting Guidelines

Best practices for effective and safe hazard reporting

What Qualifies as a Hazard?

A hazard is any condition, situation, or action that has the potential to cause injury, illness, or property damage. Examples include:

  • Unstable ground or structural issues
  • Gas or toxic chemical presence
  • Equipment malfunction or damage
  • Inadequate personal protective equipment (PPE)
  • Improper safety procedures or non-compliance
  • Environmental hazards (temperature, noise, dust)
  • Near-miss incidents
  • Unsafe work practices or conditions

How to Describe Hazards Clearly

Effective hazard descriptions help our team respond appropriately:

  • Be specific: Instead of "bad condition," describe "cracked support beam in Level 2"
  • Include details: Mention size, color, odor, sound, or any distinguishing features
  • Describe potential impact: Who could be affected and what could happen
  • Provide context: Was this an isolated incident or ongoing issue?
  • Include photos: Visual documentation is invaluable (when safe to capture)
  • Note patterns: Has this occurred before in the same location?

Timing & Urgency

Report hazards as soon as safely possible:

  • Critical/High: Report immediately - can be submitted verbally to supervisors first, then documented here
  • Medium: Report within 24 hours
  • Low: Report within 1 week
  • Timely reporting enables faster corrective action and prevents incidents
  • Document the exact date and time observed, not the time of reporting

Safety & Compliance Reminders

  • Never endanger yourself: If reporting requires entering a hazardous area, inform a supervisor first
  • Follow chain of command: Critical issues should also be reported to immediate supervisors
  • Use appropriate PPE: Always wear required safety equipment when investigating hazards
  • Document evidence: Keep photos, videos, or samples (when safe) to support your report
  • Accuracy matters: Provide truthful, factual information to help us assess risk properly
  • No retaliation: You are protected from retaliation for submitting hazard reports in good faith
  • Follow up: Check your dashboard for status updates and collaborate with safety personnel

Why Accurate Reporting Matters

Your reports directly impact mining safety:

  • Enables identification of unsafe patterns and trends
  • Helps prioritize resource allocation for hazard mitigation
  • Creates documentation for regulatory compliance
  • Supports continuous improvement in safety procedures
  • Protects you and your coworkers from injury or illness
  • Demonstrates commitment to workplace safety culture
